The department's funding and the police chief's tenure are directly tied to these metrics.<br><br><b>Success Metrics:</b> Reduction in reported crime incidents (overall and by category), improved response times to emergency calls, and positive public sentiment scores (measured through social media analysis and citizen surveys).<br><br><b>Scenario:</b><br><br>Precog has identified two potential strategies for deploying patrol units during the upcoming month. Both strategies involve allocating resources to different areas of the city based on predicted crime hotspots. However, they differ in their approach to data analysis and resource allocation.
<br>
<b>Option A: Standard Predictive Modeling with Broad Deployment</b><br><br>Precog utilizes the existing, less sophisticated predictive model, relying primarily on historical crime data and demographic information. Patrol units are deployed across a broader area, with less targeted focus on specific hotspots.<br><br>*   <b>Operational Drawbacks:</b> This strategy is projected to result in a smaller reduction in crime incidents compared to Option A. Response times will remain at current levels, and public sentiment scores are unlikely to improve significantly. These incidents are typically short-lived and do not require hospitalization, and are difficult to directly attribute to police presence.
